# Careers in Cybersecurity

## Overview
Cybersecurity offers multiple career paths, each focusing on different
aspects of protecting, monitoring, and testing systems and networks.

## Common Roles

- **Security Analyst**: Responsible for implementing and monitoring security measures across an organization to protect systems from cyber attacks.

- **Security Engineer**: Designs, develops, and implements security solutions using threat intelligence and vulnerability data.

- **Incident Responder**: Responds efficiently to security breaches by investigating incidents, containing threats, and helping organizations improve their response plans.

- **Digital Forensics Examiner**: Focuses on collecting, preserving, and analyzing digital evidence to support investigations and legal proceedings.

- **Malware Analyst**: Analyzes malicious software to understand its behavior, functionality, and impact, and documents findings in detailed reports.

- **Penetration Tester**: Tests the security of systems and applications by attempting to identify and exploit vulnerabilities in an authorized and controlled manner.

## Skills Required
- Strong understanding of networking and operating systems
- Knowledge of security tools, techniques, and frameworks
- Problem-solving, analytical thinking, and attention to detail

## Key Takeaway
Cybersecurity offers many career options, and choosing a path early
helps in building focused skills and long-term expertise.
